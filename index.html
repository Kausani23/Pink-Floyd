Discography

The Dark Side of the Moon

This is their eighth studio album, released on 1st March 1973. Its themes explore conflict, greed, time, and mental illness, the latter partly inspired by the deteriorating health of founding member Syd Barrett, who left in 1968.

Ummagumma

This is their fourth album, released on 7 November 1969. The first disc consists of live recordings from concerts at Mothers Club in Birmingham and the College of Commerce in Manchester, while the second contains solo compositions by each member of the band recorded at Abbey Road Studios.

The Wall

This is their eleventh studio album, released on 30 November 1979 in UK and in US. Pink's life begins with the loss of his father and continues with abuse from his schoolteachers, an overprotective mother, and the breakdown of his marriage; all contribute to his eventual self-imposed isolation from society, symbolised by a wall.

Wish You Were Here

This is their ninth studio album, released on 12 September 1975 in UK and a day later in US, their first American release for the label. The song "Shine On You Crazy Diamond" is a tribute to Pink Floyd founder Syd Barrett, who left seven years earlier due to mental health problems.

The Division Bell

This is their fourteenth studio album, released on 28 March 1994 UK and on 4 April US.This album was written mostly by David Gilmour and Rick Wright and features Wright's first lead vocal since TDSOTM.

This is their tenth studio album, released on 23 January 1977 in UK and US. It is a concept album that provides a scathing critique of the social-political conditions of late 1970s Britain.
